About Me

I'm a technical writer currently in the semiconductor field, where I document hardware and software solutions for engineers. I love to get my hands on new products, figure out how they work, and delve into the nuance of how to use it. Translating that experience into guides is my passion. I take pride in writing content for developers that gets them back into coding and making solutions instead of poring over Stack Overflow. I also enjoy coding in C# because I feel that a technical writer should have a strong command of English as well as the code they document. And as AI becomes ever more relevant, documentation needs to be flexible to account for all readers, whether they be humans or chatbots. In my experience, I've always yearned for opportunities to learn new skills and tinker with new technologies. In my career so far, I've learned about:

  • Semiconductor design and development
  • C# and VBA programming
  • Structured authoring frameworks, such as DITA
  • And much more!
If having an inquisitive, detail-oriented, and customer-minded technical writer join your team, please feel free to contact me.

Experience

Teradyne

Technical Writer

At Teradyne, I wrote documentation for various hardware and software products that explained how to perform complex electrical engineering functions. I wrote release notes, programming guides, design guidelines, syntax references, and more to ensure customers across the globe could operate the system. I documented solutions in unstructured FrameMaker as well as DITA and worked with engineers constantly to support continuous content updates and tight release schedules. I also peer-reviewed content from other writers and worked with offline and online publishing pipelines.
